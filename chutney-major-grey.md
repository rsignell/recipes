# Major Grey's Chutney 
Makes about 3 cups. 

## Ingredients
* **Firm mango (cubed):** 495 g
* **Apple cider vinegar:** 148 g 
* **Garlic (minced):** 7 g
* **Lime (peeled, deseeded, diced):** 22 g 
* **Red onion (diced):** 113 g 
* **Golden raisins (sultanas):** 72.5 g
* **Fresh ginger root (grated):** 8 g 
* **Dark brown sugar:** 165 g
* **White sugar:** 100 g
* **Molasses (unsulphured):** 84.5 g
* **Cinnamon stick:** 0.5 stick
* **Yellow mustard seeds:** 0.5 tsp
* **Red chilli flakes (or Serrano chilli, minced):** 0.5 tsp
* **Ground coriander:** 0.5 tsp
* **Ground cardamom:** 0.5 tsp
* **Salt:** 0.25 tsp
* **Ground black pepper:** 0.5 tsp
* **Ground nutmeg:** 0.5 tsp
* **Ground cloves:** 0.15 tsp

## Procedure
1. **Preparation:** In a heavy-bottomed, non-reactive saucepan, combine all ingredients. 
5. **Thermal Reduction:** Heat over medium until rolling boil (approximately 212°F). Once this temperature is reached, immediately reduce the heat source to maintain a low, steady simmer—ideally keeping the mixture between 195°F and 200°F.
6. **Concentration:** Maintain the simmer, uncovered, for 45 to 60 minutes. It is critical to stir periodically to ensure even heat distribution and prevent the sugars from scorching at the base of the pan. As the water evaporates, the mixture will undergo a physical change, thickening into a dark, syrupy consistency.
7. **Cooling and Maturation:** Remove the saucepan from the heat. Extract and discard the remnants of the cinnamon stick. Carefully transfer the hot chutney into sterilised glass jars. Allow the jars to sit open until the contents reach ambient room temperature, then seal them tightly. For optimal flavour development, it is advisable to let the sealed jars mature in the refrigerator for at least 48 hours prior to consumption.
