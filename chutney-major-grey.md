# Major Grey's Chutney 

## Ingredients
* **Firm mango (cubed):** 495 g
* **Apple cider vinegar:** 148 g 
* **Garlic (minced):** 7 g
* **Lime (peeled, deseeded, diced):** 22 g 
* **Red onion (diced):** 113 g 
* **Golden raisins (sultanas):** 72.5 g
* **Fresh ginger root (grated):** 8 g 
* **Dark brown sugar:** 165 g
* **White sugar:** 100 g
* **Molasses (unsulphured):** 84.5 g
* **Cinnamon stick:** 0.5 stick
* **Yellow mustard seeds:** 0.5 tsp
* **Red chilli flakes (or Serrano chilli, minced):** 0.5 tsp
* **Ground coriander:** 0.5 tsp
* **Ground cardamom:** 0.5 tsp
* **Salt:** 0.25 tsp
* **Ground black pepper:** 0.5 tsp
* **Ground nutmeg:** 0.5 tsp
* **Ground cloves:** 0.15 tsp

## Procedure
1. **Preparation of the Liquid Base:** In a heavy-bottomed, non-reactive saucepan, combine the apple cider vinegar, dark brown sugar, white sugar, and molasses. Place the vessel over moderate heat on the hob. Agitate the mixture gently but consistently until the saccharides are fully dissolved into the acid.
2. **Aromatic Integration:** Introduce the minced garlic, diced lime, diced red onion, grated ginger root, and the complete suite of dry spices into the liquid matrix.
3. **Addition of Primary Solids:** Incorporate the cubed firm mango and the golden raisins into the saucepan, stirring to ensure all organic material is coated by the liquid base.
4. **Thermal Reduction:** Increase the thermal input to bring the mixture to a rolling boil (approximately 212°F). Once this temperature is reached, immediately reduce the heat source to maintain a low, steady simmer—ideally keeping the mixture between 195°F and 200°F.
5. **Concentration:** Maintain the simmer, uncovered, for 45 to 60 minutes. It is critical to stir periodically to ensure even heat distribution and prevent the sugars from scorching at the base of the pan. As the ambient water evaporates, the mixture will undergo a physical change, thickening into a dark, syrupy consistency.
6. **Cooling and Maturation:** Remove the saucepan from the heat. Extract and discard the remnants of the cinnamon stick. Carefully transfer the hot chutney into sterilised glass jars. Allow the jars to sit open until the contents reach ambient room temperature, then seal them tightly. For optimal chemical integration and flavour development, it is advisable to let the sealed jars mature in the refrigerator for at least 48 hours prior to consumption.
