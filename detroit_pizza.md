TL;DR: This modified Detroit-style pizza uses a 50/50 blend of King Arthur flours and a triple-cheese mix (Muenster, Sharp White Cheddar, Mozzarella) to simulate authentic Wisconsin brick cheese.
1. The 50/50 Pizza Dough
• King Arthur All-Purpose Flour: 140.5g
• King Arthur Bread Flour: 140.5g
• Water (Room Temp): 246g
• Sugar: 6g (1 ½ tsp)
• Instant Yeast: 5g (1 ½ tsp)
• Fine Sea Salt: 4.5g (¾ tsp)
• Extra-Virgin Olive Oil: 13g (1 tbsp) for the pan
2. The "Brick-Style" Cheese Blend (280g Total)
• Muenster (e.g., Cabot): 140g (shredded or 1/4 inch cubes)
• Sharp White Cheddar (e.g., Cabot): 70g (shredded or 1/4 inch cubes)
• Whole Milk Mozzarella: 70g (shredded)
3. The Herbaceous Tomato Sauce
• Crushed Tomatoes: 245g (1 cup)
• Extra-Virgin Olive Oil: 13g (1 tbsp)
• Fresh Basil (chopped): 3g (1 tbsp)
• Garlic: 1 clove, minced
• Dried Oregano: 1g (1 tsp)
• Dried Basil: 1g (1 tsp)
• Sugar: 2g (½ tsp)
• Salt: 1.5g (¼ tsp)
• Black Pepper: 1g (½ tsp)
Preparation Steps
1. Mix & Autolyse: Whisk the flours, yeast, and sugar. Add water and mix on low for 2 minutes. Cover and let rest (autolyse) for 10 minutes.
2. Knead: Add the salt. Knead on medium speed for 8 minutes until the dough is satiny and clears the sides of the bowl.
3. Pan Prep: Spray a 13x9 inch pan with non-stick spray, then brush with 13g of olive oil.
4. Relax & Stretch: Place dough in the pan and let rest, covered, for 15 minutes. Press the dough into the corners with oiled fingertips.
5. Triple Rise: Cover and let rise at room temperature until tripled in volume (approx. 3 hours).
6. Assemble: Heat oven to 500°F. Spread the cheese blend edge-to-edge. Spoon the sauce in three parallel "racing stripes" over the cheese.
7. Bake: Place on the lowest rack for 15 minutes.
8. Rest: Let the pizza sit in the pan for 5 minutes before removing to prevent the cheese crown from collapsing.
